Academic Tips
for High School Students

Author: Clara Singson

Academic burnout is a universal experience for us, students. The constant strain and pressure put on us just for a high grade are, quite honestly, insane. School drowns us with work and assignments, it’s almost impossible to keep things up! That being said, here are some tips from our team to help you get through your school life:



1. Take notes

Taking notes can be hard, especially when the teacher goes at a fast pace, but even just taking the key concepts help loads! Writing down notes can help with your memorization and understanding of the topic. You don’t have to copy them word for word, you can always use your own!


2. Know your learning styles Every student is different—

we all have our own styles and our own paces. Getting to know your learning style can make a huge impact on your grades. There are four predominant learning styles: visual, auditory, reader/writer, or kinaesthetic. Find out what works best for you!


3. Keep a junk notebook With our overflowing workload

It’s important to keep track of the tasks we have yet to do. Whatever you can think of, you can write it down here— assignments, tasks, ideas, etc. Writing it down will help you avoid forgetting these things and will help you keep track.


4. Take care of yourself Forgetting to take care of yourself is where academic burnout begins to set. It’s okay to get tired, it’s okay to take breaks. Just don’t exhaust yourself to the point that you’re going to break. After all, the reason we continue to learn and study is for our future. What would that future be without you?



That’s all we have for today’s study tips! Team Kaleidoscope is always gonna be here, rooting for you~
