The Pandemic and I

Author: Yzabel Espino

During the pandemic, It took me long before I was even able to process everything going on.
I was confused, scared, and I didn't know what to do. I spent the first few weeks of lockdown all happy
and excited because I just thought "oh hey, atleast we get a few weeks off of school."

It hit me slowly, the feeling of loneliness and fear overwhelmed me because of everything going on, I wanted it to end quickly. I consider myself as a person who enjoys good company, and being kept away from physical contact with family and friends made it hard to me to cope.


I had to face a strong feeling of loneliness. And then online classes came, I was quite nervous, yet excited because I can atleast interact with my schoolmates, even if I was just sitting in front of a laptop screen. Being surrounded with nothing but walls of screens overwhelmed me once again. It was hard for me to adjust.

But even if the past few years were rough, I was able to get plenty of life lessons from it, and I'm thankful that I left my comfort zone and explored new things, and there were many people like my parents and close friends who helped me get through rough patches and finally adapt and embrace change.

Yes, change is scary. But change is inevitable, with patience and a good grasp of yourself, overcoming change is possible. But we have to start with ourselves. In order to adapt to change, we must embrace it. And along the way, we will realize how change can also bring good opportunities for us to meet new people, and explore new things.
